Houdini 22 × ComfyUI — A VFX × AI Hybrid Workflow with Gaussian Splats from Shibuya

Spain-based VFX artist hectorVFX shared the results of a new VFX × AI hybrid workflow experiment on X, built on Gaussian Splats (3DGS) of the Shibuya scramble crossing provided by Locahun 3D. The technique combines Houdini 22 with ComfyUI.

01 The trigger: Houdini 22, a “monster”

According to hectorVFX, the release of Houdini 22 is what pushed this hybrid workflow further. He describes Houdini 22 as “an absolute monster” for handling Gaussian Splats, saying this version's capabilities are hard to believe. The starting point for this workflow is treating 3DGS not just as background material, but as a layer you can genuinely work with inside a 3D DCC tool.

02 How the workflow flows

The X post itself only described a broad “three-stage” version of this. But a 33-second screen recording hectorVFX sent over afterward shows a much more concrete sequence of steps. The interesting part: the environment (Shibuya's 3DGS) and the subject (the car) are built through completely separate pipelines, and both stay as Gaussian Splats right up until they're merged into one scene in Houdini. Nothing here is a 2D image composite — these are two point clouds merged in 3D space.

① Houdini 22: loading Shibuya's 3DGS natively

The Shibuya scramble crossing 3DGS data provided by Locahun 3D is loaded directly into Houdini 22 (Indie license, build 22.0.368). The recording shows a scene graph running bakegsplat → transform → normal → merge — the native Gaussian Splatting node set Houdini 22 newly ships with. This step treats the 3DGS not as “a point cloud you can only look at,” but as geometry with normals that can be combined with other nodes.

② ComfyUI: generating the car as a 3DGS straight from an image

The white sports car isn't a photo composite — it was generated from scratch as a 3D Gaussian Splat inside ComfyUI. The workflow tab visible in the recording is named ER_TripoSplat_Live. A KSampler runs 20 denoising steps (cfg 3.0 / sampler: dpmpp_2m / scheduler: simple / denoise 1.0), the result is decoded by a TripoSplat Decode node into a splat with num_gaussians: 300,000, and a Create 3D File (from Splat) node writes it out. In other words, the car isn't “a 2D image turned 3D by AI” — it's generated directly as 3D Gaussians from the diffusion model's latent space.

What is TripoSplat?It's a family of ComfyUI custom nodes that takes Tripo-style image/text-to-3D models and outputs Gaussian Splats instead of a mesh. Because the output is already a splat, it can merge natively into the Houdini 3DGS scene from step ① — that's the crux of this whole workflow.

③ Compositing in Houdini: two 3DGS assets become one scene

The car splat generated in ② is loaded into Houdini and combined with the Shibuya scene from ① via a merge node. The recording shows the Transform SOP's pivot, translate, scale and rotate values being adjusted by hand to seat the car on the crosswalk. At this point, two datasets with entirely different origins — a real-world scan and an AI-generated asset — become a single 3D scene.

④ Camera, lighting and the final render

A cam1 camera is set up in the composited scene, with motion blur and depth-of-field parameters (Shutter Time 0.5 / Focus Distance 5 / F-stop 5.6) dialed in for the render. The finishing pass adds the night-time neon reflections and wet-asphalt lighting grade that produces the final shot shown at the top of this article.

05 About the data used

The Gaussian Splats of the Shibuya scramble crossing that anchor this workflow were provided by Kou Nakamura, founder of Locahun 3D. Data actually scanned on foot with PortalCam ended up, across an ocean, in another creator's hands for a new piece of work — a genuinely nice outcome to see.

06 Summary

With Houdini 22, the environment is maturing to treat Gaussian Splats as more than “something to just look at” — as a layer you can properly control inside a 3D scene. Locahun 3D's scan data can serve as the foundation for hybrid VFX × AI workflows like this one. If you're experimenting or producing something similar, feel free to reach out.
