LOCAHUN 3D — LOCATION SCOUT EST. 2026
CHAPTER 01 00:01:00
CH. 01 The Gap — the step that fell short

An expression that almost never came to be was
left behind on set after the shoot was over.

01 / SCOUT

Limited budget

Large-crew scouting and constant schedule juggling.
Budget eaten away by repeated site visits and checks.
Before you even shoot, your options are already narrowed.

02 / SHARE

Lack of resources leads to mistakes

Conveying “what the shot will look like” between director,
cinematographer, art and producer with only words or rough
sketches has its limits. One person’s gap becomes a fatal mistake.

03 / TIME

Time constraints

The tests you really want — focal length, blocking, the direction of light.
On set, the time to try them is measured in minutes.
Most of the possibilities vanish untested.

THE GAP P. 02 / 07
CHAPTER 02 00:02:00
CH. 02 The Method — reinventing shoot prep

Bring the location home, 3D space and all.
A 3DGS capture service &
an offline-capable digital web application

01 / CAMERA
Virtual camera

Sensor / focal length / aspect
WB / pan, tilt, roll.
Lens simulation equivalent to the real gear.

02 / FIGURE
Add figure

Place a pre-posed human model.
Blocking and eyelines lock in, and
the storyboard builds itself on the spot.

03 / MULTI-CAM
Multi-camera save

Save a camera for each cut.
Adjust as if on set during the meeting, and
decide shooting order and camera height digitally.

04 / ANIMATION
Camera animation

Connect saved cameras to generate a path.
Export straight to MP4 and check the moving shot at once.

05 / MEASURE
Distance measurement

Snaps precisely to the splat surface.
True-to-reality dimensions even in large scenes.

06 / LIGHTING
Environmental lighting

Morning, midday, evening, night, rain.
Instantly test the look of the whole scene at each time of day.

07 / GRID
Composition grid

Rule of thirds, golden, diagonal, AE-compatible safe, custom.
Multiple overlays at once.

08 / BURN-IN
FHD burn-in

Export at 1920×1080 as standard.
Metadata sits in the frame outside the image, leaving the shot untouched.

09 / OFFLINE
Offline save

Even where there’s no signal, open it in Chrome or Safari and
check it on the spot, on any device.

METHOD & TOOLS P. 03 / 06
CHAPTER 03 00:03:00
CH. 03 The Outcome — what happens on set

Try out your framing checks digitally

DIRECTOR
Hand over the shot as an image, not words

Share shot breakdown, composition and camera position with the whole crew in the same image before the shoot.
Direction gets sharper, and “unexpected shots” on the day go down.

D.O.P.
Lens choice is settled at the desk

14 / 24 / 35 / 50 / 85mm — there’s no time to try them all on set.
Narrow them down first with Locahun 3D, and focus on shooting on the day.

PRODUCER
Pitch decks that connect to the set

Client pitches, the rationale behind the call sheet, scout reports —
metadata-stamped JPEGs are faster than explaining, stronger than arguing.

A. D.
Scouting becomes a repeatable task

Drop a JPEG from a past shot and return to the same spot.
Handoffs, retakes, sequels — never lost again.
